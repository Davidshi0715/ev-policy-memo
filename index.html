Background

Beijing is experiencing rapid growth in its electric vehicle fleet, expected to reach 1.5 million by 2025. Although there are 114,000 public charging stations, their uneven distribution and challenges such as high land rent and grid constraints have limited accessibility, especially in dense urban districts.

Stakeholders

The Beijing government, State Grid Corporation, EV owners, private charging companies, real estate developers, and local residents all play crucial roles in policy implementation.

Policy Options

  • Park and Charge: Subsidize private companies to build stations in public spaces, with capped land rents.
  • Home Powered: Renovate old residential grids to support home charging stations.
  • Power on Wheels: Promote mobile charging robots offering on-demand services.

Recommendation

The Home Powered solution is recommended for its sustainability and long-term benefits, including lower costs for EV owners and greater compatibility with future technologies.
